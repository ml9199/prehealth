What Is the Pre-Health Track?

In many healthcare specialties, including medicine and dentistry, the admission requirements for four-year doctoral programs are largely similar and have been standardized across most professional institutions. Regardless of the undergraduate institution attended, students should plan to complete the courses listed on the requirement section. The pre-health track is a set of courses and experiences that prepare undergraduate students for medical school applications. It requires careful planning starting from freshman year, including science prerequisites, clinical experience, research, and a competitive MCAT score.

Course Requirements

  • General Biology I & II (with Labs)
  • General Chemistry I & II (with Labs)
  • Organic Chemistry I & II (with Labs)
  • General Physics I & II (with Labs)
  • Biochemistry
  • Calculus
  • English/Writing

The above courses are the minimum requirements for admission to most medical and dental programs. Some schools may require additional courses. Also, there might be additional classes you have to take depends on your major. Please plan accordingly.
