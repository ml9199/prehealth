Application Components

Primary Application (AMCAS)

Includes your transcript, GPA, MCAT score, a list of up to 15 activities (research, volunteering, shadowing, work), and your personal statement. Verification takes 4–6 weeks — submit early.

Secondary Applications

Each school sends its own secondary with short school-specific essays. Most ask about diversity, why that school, and experiences with adversity. Pre-writing common prompts over the summer saves time.

Personal Statement

Your personal statement is 5,300 characters — about one page. It should tell the story of why medicine, not repeat your resume. The strongest essays are specific and show self-reflection.

  • Hook: Open with a specific moment that drew you to medicine.
  • Development: Connect experiences to insights about the field.
  • Reflection: What did you learn about yourself?
  • Looking forward: How does medicine fit your values and goals?

Tip: Avoid "ever since I was young" or "I want to help people." Be specific.

Interview

Being invited to an interview means your application is competitive. The interview is about fit.

Traditional

One-on-one with a faculty member or student. Prepare for "tell me about yourself" and ethical scenarios.

MMI

Multiple mini stations, ~8 minutes each. Tests communication and critical thinking. Practice is essential.

What You Should Know

Application Timeline

  • MCAT — Jan to Apr (Junior Year)
  • AMCAS Opens — May
  • Submit Primary — Jun to Jul
  • Secondary Applications — Aug to Sep
  • Interviews — Oct to Mar
  • Decisions — Mar to Apr

Letters of Recommendation

Most schools require 3–4 letters: at least one from a science professor, one from a non-science professor, and one from a physician you've shadowed. Ask early — by the end of junior spring.
